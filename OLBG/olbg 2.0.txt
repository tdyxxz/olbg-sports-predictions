CRICKET-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS

Pitch and weather conditions are more impactful in cricket than in any other sport in this series - a spinner on a turning pitch or a pace bowler on a seaming surface can single-handedly determine a match outcome regardless of team form. Always weight pitch report confirmation heavily in all four market scores
All-rounders are structurally the best Man of the Match candidates in cricket - they have two pathways to impact compared to one for specialists. Always elevate all-rounder scores in this market and identify the primary all-rounder candidate for each team before scoring any other player
Rain interruptions disproportionately favour teams with higher strike rate top orders - in shortened matches the team that can accelerate in powerplay overs holds structural advantage. Flag any weather risk in the internal data before finalising predictions
T20 format produces higher variance in individual batting markets than ODI or Test - a single over can end an opener's innings regardless of form. Reduce Top Batsman confidence by one tier in T20 if pitch is likely to assist bowling in powerplay overs
Openers in ODI and Test formats hold longer innings advantage than T20 - adjust batting position scoring accordingly per format
Head-to-head records in cricket are particularly meaningful because bowling attacks and batting lineups tend to hold consistent strengths that specific opponents can exploit repeatedly across seasons
Man of the Match in T20 cricket is frequently won by bowlers who restrict opponents in death overs or spinners who take multiple wickets in the middle overs - never over-weight batting candidates when a specialist bowler has an obvious pitch matchup advantage
Venue-specific spin records are among the most consistent edges in cricket prediction - certain grounds produce spinning tracks in nearly every match played there. Identify these venues internally and weight the spin-bowling matchup score accordingly

GOLF-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS

Strokes gained approach is the single most predictive metric for tournament winners - a player consistently gaining strokes on approach across multiple events represents a more reliable selection than a player whose recent results are driven by a hot putter, which is highly volatile week-to-week
Golf profitability is damaged quickly by forcing exposure across outright, first round leader, and multiple regional markets in the same event - when the edge is marginal, keep the card short and prioritise the single market with the clearest statistical alignment
Course fit is uniquely powerful in golf compared to other sports - the match between a player's ball-striking profile and specific course demands can override ranking and general form entirely. A player ranked 60th in the world who has finished top 5 at this specific course twice in three years is a more credible selection than a top-10 player with no course history
Weather is the highest impact external variable in golf - wind above 20mph fundamentally changes which player profiles succeed, strongly favouring low ball-flight players, links-experienced players, and those with superior course management over pure power hitters
First round leader is structurally different from all other golf markets - it is won disproportionately by players with early tee times in deteriorating conditions, players on a hot putting streak, and players with a documented history of fast starts. Ranking and general form are secondary factors for this market specifically
Regional markets are not simply smaller versions of the outright market - a player can be the highest-ranked European in the field but have a poor course fit relative to a lower-ranked European whose profile matches the course perfectly. Always score regional markets independently rather than defaulting to the highest world ranking
Value selections are the profit engine in golf tournament betting - outright winners at major events frequently come from outside the top 5 favourites. A player with strong course history, rising strokes gained trajectory, and a recent top 10 represents better long-term value than short-priced favourites regardless of ranking
Cut-making consistency is more predictive for top 6 markets than raw scoring potential - a player who regularly finishes between 15th and 30th with few missed cuts is a stronger top 6 selection than a volatile player capable of both winning and missing cuts
