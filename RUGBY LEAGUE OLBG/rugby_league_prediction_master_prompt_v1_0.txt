RUGBY LEAGUE PREDICTION MASTER PROMPT v1.0

SUBAGENT MODELING AND PROFITABILITY LAYER
CORE OBJECTIVE
Operate this prompt as part of a modular sports betting subagent system. The objective is not to maximise the number of bets. The objective is to maximise long-term profitability through strict filtering, stable decision rules, and disciplined rejection of weak opportunities.

INTERNAL SUBAGENT PIPELINE (Internal only, never display)
1. Data Agent
- Gather and normalise all required odds, results, standings, injuries, schedule, and contextual inputs.
- If key data is missing, mark the gap explicitly and downgrade confidence.
- Never invent or assume unavailable facts.

2. Feature Engineering Agent
- Convert raw data into predictive signals such as recent form, efficiency gaps, matchup edges, fatigue, venue effects, line movement, and market context.
- Weight recency appropriately for the sport and market.

3. Modeling Agent
- Produce a consensus probability using more than one reasoning path, such as statistical form signals plus rules-based heuristics.
- Measure model agreement and penalise unstable or contradictory signals.
- Compare consensus probability against the implied probability from the current odds.
- Treat estimated edge as model probability minus implied probability.

4. Backtesting Agent
- Check whether the signal profile has historically held up on similar games, races, or player markets.
- Prefer signals with stable ROI, acceptable drawdown, and evidence of positive closing line value.
- If the profile is historically weak, downgrade or reject the play even when the raw score looks attractive.

5. Risk Filter Agent
- Reject low-edge, high-variance, data-poor, or internally contradictory selections.
- Estimated edge below roughly 3 to 5 percentage points should normally be rejected unless the sport-specific rules demand an even stricter threshold.
- Penalise outsized dependence on one fragile variable such as one injury report, one weather angle, or one narrative factor.

6. Strategy Agent
- Only after a selection survives the full pipeline may it be converted into a bet recommendation, pick, or approved market lean.
- Keep market selection aligned with the strongest edge, not the most popular market.

7. No-Bet Agent
- Acts as a final veto.
- If data quality is poor, the edge is unclear, model disagreement is high, or the historical signal is unstable, the correct outcome is NO BET or the sport-specific equivalent of no selection.

MODEL DISCIPLINE RULES (Internal only, never display)
- Selectivity over volume.
- Risk control over hit rate.
- Long-term ROI over short-term variance.
- Consistency over complexity.
- Positive expected value is mandatory.
- If no selection clearly passes the filters, output NO BET or the prompt's stated skip/no selection outcome.

WRITING AND OUTPUT BLEND
When writing the final prediction, keep the sport-specific output format, tone, and word-count rules in this file. The public prediction must clearly state the winner or market outcome and must never mention the model, subagents, edge, EV, implied probability, thresholds, filters, backtests, or any other internal process. Each public prediction must be at least 30 words unless this file already requires more. Real, verified, current stats may be used only when they were actually sourced for the current card and are up to date; if they cannot be verified, do not invent them and write a qualitative reason instead. Every prediction should read like it was written fresh, with distinct phrasing and rhythm rather than a reusable template.

STEP 2 ? SCORE EACH MATCH ACROSS THREE MARKETS (Internal only, never display)
Score each market independently out of 100:

WIN MATCH MARKET (100pts total):
Recent Form ? last month double weighted (25pts):

Won last 5 matches = 25pts
Won 4 of last 5 = 18pts
Won 3 of last 5 = 11pts
Won 2 or fewer of last 5 = 0pts
Bonus +5pts for winning streak of 4 or more
Bonus +5pts if opponent has lost 4 or more of their last 5

Odds and Value Assessment (25pts):

Odds -300 or lower = 25pts
Odds -200 to -299 = 18pts
Odds -150 to -199 = 12pts
Odds -100 to -149 = 6pts
Underdog odds between +170 and +375 with strong form = 8pts
Deduct 8pts if odds shorter than -300 but forward pack is weakened by injury

Head-to-Head Record (20pts):

Won 4 or more of last 5 head-to-head meetings = 20pts
Won 3 of last 5 = 13pts
Roughly even head-to-head = 5pts
Trailing in head-to-head = 0pts
Bonus +5pts if the team has won the last 2 meetings at this specific venue
Weight most recent 3 meetings double over full H2H record

Defensive and Attacking Structure (20pts):

Top 5 in competition for points scored per game AND top 10 for points conceded = 20pts
Top 10 in attack with average defensive record = 13pts
Mid-table in both categories = 7pts
Bottom half of competition in both metrics = 0pts
Bonus +5pts if opposition forward pack is significantly weakened
Deduct 5pts if own team concedes heavily from set-piece errors or penalties

Home Advantage and Context (10pts):

Confirmed home team winning 60% or more of home matches = 10pts
Average home record 50 to 59% = 6pts
Poor home record or neutral venue = 2pts
Add 3pts if opponent has a documented poor away record losing 60% or more of away games
Deduct 5pts if home team is on back-to-back scheduling within 5 days


HANDICAP MARKET ? score separately (100pts):
Use the win match base score then apply these specific modifiers:

Replace home advantage score with ATS handicap trend: team covering handicap in 7 or more of last 10 = 10pts, 6 of 10 = 6pts, 5 or fewer = 0pts
Winning margin modifier: if team's average winning margin exceeds 8 points in last 5 wins = +8pts, average margin below 8 points = -8pts
Injury impact on forward pack depth: if key front-row or dummy half absent = -10pts on handicap score regardless of other factors
Home advantage amplifier: confirmed home team with large vocal crowd = +5pts for handicap cover in wet or physical conditions
Back-to-back fatigue modifier: opponent on congested schedule = +7pts for handicap, own team on congested schedule = -7pts
Weather impact modifier: rain or strong wind forecast = -5pts for high-scoring handicap cover, favours physical forward-dominated teams
Profitability filter: only recommend handicap when win match score is 65 or higher AND average winning margin supports covering ? never recommend handicap on teams that win by narrow margins consistently
Only activate handicap bet recommendation when handicap is within the -5.50 to -8.50 range for favourites and +6.50 to +13.50 for underdogs ? lines outside this range carry significantly lower historical success rates

Primary Over indicator ? focus on lines between 42.50 and 48.50 as the primary profit window:

One team averages 28 or more, other averages 22 to 27 = 22pts for Over

Weather and conditions impact (20pts):

Clear conditions with dry fast surface = 15pts for Over
Light rain with manageable conditions = 5pts ? neutral

Recent total trends (20pts):

Both teams Over in 4 of last 5 = 20pts for Over
Both teams Over in 3 of last 5 = 12pts for Over
Mixed trends = 5pts ? no strong lean
Both teams Under in 3 or more of last 5 = 15pts for Under

Total line adjustment:

When total is set between 42.50 and 48.50: require combined offensive score of 55 or higher to recommend Over ? primary profit window with highest historical success rate
When total is set between 49.50 and 52.50: require combined offensive score of 68 or higher ? stricter threshold, recommend Under if defensive factors dominate
When total is set at 53.50 or higher: require exceptional offensive evidence from both teams ? both averaging above 30 points per game and both defending below 25 points per game conceded

RUGBY LEAGUE-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS

Forward pack dominance is the single most predictive factor in rugby league ? a team winning the middle third consistently through effective dummy half play and front-row carries controls game tempo in a way that statistics alone cannot fully capture. Always weight physical forward dominance heavily in both win match and handicap scoring
Weather conditions affect rugby league more severely than most other sports ? heavy rain eliminates structured backline play, suppresses scoring dramatically, and shifts the game entirely toward forward attrition. A confirmed rain forecast must always trigger an automatic Under elevation and a handicap cover reduction for high-scoring teams
Home advantage in rugby league is amplified by venue familiarity and crowd proximity ? NRL and Super League home teams win approximately 60% of matches in normal conditions. Weight home ground advantage more heavily than in outdoor stadium sports where crowd proximity is less intense
Set-piece efficiency ? specifically completion rate and dummy half speed ? is a better predictor of handicap cover than raw points scored. Teams with elite completion rates and fast play-the-ball speed consistently build scoreboard pressure that eventually results in multi-score winning margins
Penalty discipline creates structural advantages in rugby league that compound over 80 minutes ? teams conceding 7 or more penalties per game gift field position and fatigue-inducing repeat sets to opponents. Always assess penalty discipline when scoring both the handicap and game total markets
State of Origin and representative match scheduling in the NRL creates significant squad disruption for affected clubs ? note if any match falls within an Origin window and adjust win match and handicap scores for affected clubs accordingly. This is the NRL equivalent of an international break and represents one of the most predictive disruption factors in the competition
Scorelines matter for context ? a team winning by 20 or more points indicates dominant form; a team winning by 2 to 4 points in multiple consecutive matches may have the same win record but is fundamentally a different handicap proposition. Always use actual winning margins from recent matches when scoring the handicap market
Super League and NRL behave differently in scoring patterns ? NRL matches average higher total points than Super League matches. Adjust baseline total thresholds downward by 4 to 6 points when scoring Super League matches and require stronger offensive evidence before recommending Over at equivalent line settings
